# Screens: 12 Spend Analytics, Supplier Scorecard and KPIs (Build-real, hero)

Flow 12 realized as screens. Source: `documentation/12-analytics.md`, data dictionary `SupplierScorecard.json` / `Budget.json` / `Commitment.json` / `CreditorLedger.json`, `model/role-permission-matrix.md`. This flow is read-only computed: the System computes from the transactional record on every commit (incremental) and at period close (full roll-up); roles only view. Covers the Management dashboard (hero), the supplier scorecard detail (two-stage scoring, AVL, CAPA-driven grade drop), spend cuts, and the savings report. No screen mutates a record; every number is defensible, sourced from the data, with no AI/ML labelling.

### S12.1 Management Dashboard (archetype: dashboard / per-role landing for Management)
- **Route:** `/analytics`
- **Roles:** Management/Approver (view aggregate, `analytics.view` in-scope); Buyer (full, `analytics.view`, `scorecard.view`, `spend.view`); Finance-Maker (AP/match KPIs, `payments.analytics.view`); Quality (quality KPIs, `analytics.view` conditional); Budget Owner (cost-center, `analytics.view` conditional). Read-only.
- **Realizes:** steps 11-13 (publish dashboards, role-scoped views); the Management landing from storyboard section 6 (scorecard + spend + KPI dashboard); the storyboard payoff beat.
- **Purpose:** the management home and the demo payoff - the loop closes visually: KPI tiles that prove the source-to-pay cycle is under control, with the genuinely system-computed numbers (OTIF, perfect order, savings vs baseline, cycle time, spend-under-management, DPO, budget commitment-vs-actual) shown truthfully and defensibly.
- **Data shown:** KPI tiles, each a computed figure with its formula on hover - OTIF two-factor (`SupplierScorecard.otifTwoFactor`, on-time AND in-full per order line) shown distinctly from perfect-order four-factor (`SupplierScorecard.perfectOrderFourFactor`: on-time AND in-full AND damage-free AND documentation-accurate, with the failing-factor breakdown); savings vs last-purchase baseline (hard savings = baseline - actual, cost avoidance reported separately, never summed); req-to-PO cycle time (SLA 7-10 days); spend-under-management % ((approved spend - maverick) / total); DPO (avg AP / COGS * 365, sourced from `CreditorLedger`); budget commitment-vs-actual (`Budget.committedAmount` vs `Budget.actualAmount` vs `Budget.availableAmount`); three-way-match exception rate (100 - first-time-match rate). Trend Badges (rolling average, slope) per tile.
- **Primary actions:** none (read-only computed; System computes, roles view).
- **Secondary actions / navigation:** Tabs for the role-scoped views (Management aggregate / Buyer full / Quality KPIs / Finance AP KPIs); drill into supplier scorecard (S12.2), spend cuts (S12.3), savings report (S12.4); time-range and period (month/quarter) filters; alert feed (price-spike > 5%, expiry, two-consecutive-below, SLA breach).
- **States to design:** loading (tile skeletons), populated, low-confidence (a tile flagged when classification coverage is below the configurable threshold; unclassified-spend % shown), FX-degraded (an "approximate base" note when convertToBase carried original amounts), error.
- **Shadcn components:** Card KPI tiles, Recharts (trend lines, bar/donut for spend), Tabs (role views), Badge (trend, confidence, alerts), Tooltip (formula on hover), no action buttons.
- **Acceptance criteria (believable done):** (1) OTIF two-factor and perfect-order four-factor are shown as distinct tiles, never conflated - the two-factor is the operational on-time-and-in-full streak, the four-factor adds damage-free and documentation-accurate and shows the failing factor (axiom A14); (2) savings shows hard savings (baseline - actual, baseline = last purchase price) separately from cost avoidance, never summed, and can be negative; (3) budget commitment-vs-actual reads committedAmount, actualAmount and availableAmount from the budget (commitment relieved to actual at GR/invoice, available reduced only at commit); (4) every tile is computed from the transactional record with its formula visible on hover (defensible number; no AI/ML labelling); (5) a tile flagged low-confidence when classification coverage is below threshold (data-health honesty).
- **Tags:** [OE3 | RL.1.2 | procurement-metrics-kpis + AB]

### S12.2 Supplier Scorecard Detail (archetype: detail / dashboard)
- **Route:** `/analytics/scorecards/:supplierId`, `/analytics/scorecards/:supplierId/:period`
- **Roles:** Buyer (full + configure weights/AVL threshold, `scorecard.view`, `scorecard.configure`); Management/Quality (view, `analytics.view`/conditional); Supplier (own scorecard only, conditional, via portal). Read-only except the Buyer configuration.
- **Realizes:** steps 3-8 (stage-1 normalize, two-factor OTIF and four-factor perfect-order, stage-2 weighted composite, compliance gate, grade band, persist AVL + streaks).
- **Purpose:** the wow screen for supplier performance - the two-stage scoring made legible: each dimension normalized 0-100, the weighted composite, the grade band, the hard compliance gate, the AVL status, and how a CAPA/NCR dropped the grade and tripped the supplier toward SUSPENDED.
- **Data shown:** `SupplierScorecard.json` for the supplier-period - qualityScore, deliveryScore, costScore, responsivenessScore (each 0-100, stage-1 normalized), weights (default delivery 40 / quality 30 / cost 20 / responsiveness 10, sum 100), composite (sum sub-score * weight), grade (A >= 90 / B >= 70 / C < 70), complianceGate (cert validity 100% + doc completeness; a hard gate), avlStatus ({Approved, Conditional, Preferred, Suspended, Disqualified}), otifTwoFactor, perfectOrderFourFactor (factor breakdown); the period time-series (rolling average, trend slope, consecutive-at-or-above and consecutive-below streak counters); the CAPA history feeding the quality dimension (`CorrectiveAction` against the supplier).
- **Primary actions:** Buyer-only configuration (`scorecard.configure`) - adjust weights (must sum to 100), the AVL threshold (default 70), and the per-dimension bands. No record mutation otherwise (the scorecard is System-computed).
- **Secondary actions / navigation:** period selector (month/quarter); dimension drill-down (the metrics behind each sub-score: defect/PPM/NCR for quality; OTD/fill-rate/OTIF for delivery; PPV for cost; RFQ turnaround/CAPA responsiveness for responsiveness); link to the source NCR/CAPA (flow 11); link to sourcing (flow 04 reads AVL).
- **States to design:** loading, populated, compliance-gate-fail (AVL capped at Conditional/Suspended despite a high composite, visibly flagged), grade-dropped (the period-over-period drop highlighted after a CAPA), suspended (AVL Suspended Badge + new-PO-blocked note), error.
- **Shadcn components:** Card (dimension sub-scores + composite), Recharts (radar/bar for the four dimensions, trend line for the time-series), Badge (grade, AVL status, compliance gate pass/fail, streak), Form (Buyer weight/threshold config), Tooltip (formula), Tabs (dimensions / OTIF vs perfect order / history).
- **Acceptance criteria (believable done):** (1) the composite is stage-2 weighted (sum of sub-score * weight, weights sum to 100, default 40/30/20/10) over the four stage-1-normalized dimensions, reproducible from the stored weights; (2) compliance is a hard eligibility gate (cert validity 100% + doc completeness), never a weighted input, and a gate FAIL caps AVL at Conditional/Suspended regardless of a high composite (axiom A15); (3) the grade bands are A >= 90 / B >= 70 / C < 70 and AVL eligible = gate PASS AND composite >= threshold (default 70), Preferred = sustained Grade A; (4) two-factor OTIF and four-factor perfect order are shown distinctly with the four-factor failing factor visible (axiom A14); (5) a closed not-effective CAPA / recurring NCR shows the consecutive-below counter ticking and the grade/AVL dropping, tripping toward SUSPENDED (CAPA-driven grade drop, axiom A11), and a Suspended supplier shows the new-PO-blocked note.
- **Tags:** [OE3 | ISO 8.4.1 | SupplierScorecard two-stage engine]

### S12.3 Spend Cuts (archetype: dashboard / comparison)
- **Route:** `/analytics/spend`
- **Roles:** Buyer (full, `spend.view`); Management (aggregate, `analytics.view` in-scope); Budget Owner (cost-center, `analytics.view` conditional). Read-only.
- **Realizes:** step 10 (compute spend cuts) and the spend portion of step 9 (spend-under-management).
- **Purpose:** the spend lens - cut by category, supplier (with parent hierarchy for concentration/risk), department/cost-center, and region/plant, with tail spend and contract-compliance/utilization, so management sees where the money goes and where leakage is.
- **Data shown:** spend cuts from the transactional record normalized to base via FX - by category (the taxonomy), by supplier (+ parent hierarchy = concentration/risk), by department/cost-center (against `Budget.costCenterId`), by region/plant (Plant-1 domestic / Plant-2 import); tail spend (~20% of spend across ~80% of suppliers); contract compliance/utilization % (on-contract / addressable, the inverse of maverick); spend-under-management %; budget commitment-vs-actual per cost-center from `Budget` / `Commitment`.
- **Primary actions:** none (read-only computed).
- **Secondary actions / navigation:** switch the cut dimension (category / supplier / dept / region); drill from a category/supplier into its lines; period and base-currency filters; link to the supplier scorecard (S12.2) and budget (cost-center).
- **States to design:** loading, populated, low-coverage (a cut flagged untrustworthy below the classification-coverage threshold, unclassified-spend % shown), FX-degraded note, error.
- **Shadcn components:** Recharts (bar/treemap/donut per cut), DataTable (drill-down lines), Card (spend-under-management, contract utilization), Badge (concentration/risk, low-coverage), Tabs (cut dimensions), no action buttons.
- **Acceptance criteria (believable done):** (1) every cut is computed from the transactional record normalized to base currency (cross-currency spend comparable, axiom A12); (2) a cut is flagged untrustworthy when classification coverage is below the configurable threshold, with unclassified-spend % shown as its own data-health figure; (3) supplier spend rolls up to the parent hierarchy for concentration/risk; (4) spend-under-management = (approved spend - maverick) / total and contract utilization is the inverse of maverick; (5) budget commitment-vs-actual per cost-center reconciles committed + actual against the allotment.
- **Tags:** [CO/P3 | procurement-metrics-kpis spend cuts]

### S12.4 Savings Report (archetype: dashboard / list)
- **Roles:** Buyer (full, `spend.view`, `analytics.view`); Management (vs target, `analytics.view` in-scope). Read-only.
- **Route:** `/analytics/savings`
- **Realizes:** step 10 (compute savings) and the price-spike alert.
- **Purpose:** the savings lens - the two-layer savings model (negotiation + comparison) against the last-purchase-price baseline, hard savings separated from cost avoidance, plus the abnormal price-spike watch.
- **Data shown:** savings computed against baseline = last purchase price - the negotiation layer (quoted-increase vs negotiated-increase) and the comparison layer (selected vs alternative quotes); hard savings = baseline - actual; cost avoidance reported in a separate column, never summed with hard savings; savings can be negative. The abnormal price-spike watch (unit price > 5% above baseline raises a market-price-watch alert, configurable threshold). The manual forecast (month/quarter * quantity) with a variance flag when actual deviates beyond a configurable band.
- **Primary actions:** none for savings (read-only computed); the manual forecast is Buyer-only (`forecast.manage`, procurement-only) and is the one editable analytics surface (month/quarter * quantity entries).
- **Secondary actions / navigation:** filter by category/supplier/period; drill into a savings line to the source quote/PO; the price-spike alert links to the supplier/item.
- **States to design:** loading, populated, negative-savings (shown, not hidden), price-spike alert surfaced, forecast-variance flagged, error.
- **Shadcn components:** DataTable (savings lines with hard / avoidance columns), Recharts (savings trend, forecast vs actual), Card (price-spike watch), Form (Buyer manual forecast), Badge (price-spike, variance), no action buttons for savings.
- **Acceptance criteria (believable done):** (1) the baseline is the last purchase price and savings use the two layers (negotiation + comparison); (2) hard savings (baseline - actual) and cost avoidance are reported in separate columns and never summed, and savings can be negative (no inflation of the number); (3) an abnormal price spike (unit price > 5% above baseline, configurable) raises a market-price-watch alert to the buyer; (4) the forecast is manual, procurement-only, month/quarter * quantity, with a variance flag beyond a configurable band; (5) every savings figure traces to its source quote/PO (defensible, no AI/ML labelling).
- **Tags:** [CO | procurement-metrics-kpis savings + AB two-layer]

### Inline edge behaviours shown on these screens (not separate screens)
- **OTIF two-vs-four-factor (A14):** shown inline on S12.1 and S12.2 as two distinct tiles/metrics, never conflated; the four-factor shows the failing factor.
- **CAPA-driven grade drop (A11):** shown inline on S12.2 as the consecutive-below counter and the period grade/AVL drop after a closed not-effective CAPA / recurring NCR.
- **Defensible-number note:** every tile/figure shows its formula on hover and traces to the transactional record; nothing is labelled AI/ML (PM honesty guardrail); the genuinely impressive capabilities are positioned truthfully (automated nearest-bucket approval, normalized landed-cost decision support, two-stage scorecard, closed CAPA loop).
- **Compliance gate (A15):** shown inline on S12.2 - a gate FAIL caps AVL despite a high composite.
- **FX / coverage degradation:** shown inline as the "approximate base" note on FX failure and the low-coverage flag below the classification-coverage threshold (data-health honesty).
