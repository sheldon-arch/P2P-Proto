# Screens: 04 Sourcing, RFQ to Award and Contract (Build-real)

Flow 04 realized as screens. Source: `documentation/04-sourcing.md`, data dictionary `RFQ.json` / `Quotation.json` / `QuotationLine.json` / `LandedCost.json` / `Contract.json` / `FrameworkAgreement.json` / `CallOffRelease.json` / `SupplierScorecard.json`, `model/role-permission-matrix.md`, state machines `approvalCompletion` (award stage) and the RFQ / Quotation / Contract status enums. The landed-cost comparison workbench (S04.4) is the #1 wow screen in the storyboard and carries the most acceptance-criteria detail.

### S04.1 Sourcing Pipeline (archetype: dashboard / per-role landing for Buyer)
- **Route:** `/sourcing`
- **Roles:** Procurement/Buyer (act: `rfq.create`, `quote.compare`, `supplier.select`); Approver (view award-stage items, act on award approval); Administrator (view all). Management view-only on award recommendations.
- **Realizes:** narrative step 1 (approved requisition ready for sourcing, handler assignment); the Buyer landing from storyboard section 6.
- **Purpose:** the buyer's home for sourcing work. Answers "what needs sourcing, what RFQs are open, what is waiting on me" in the first 30 seconds. Three work buckets: requisitions awaiting sourcing (FULLY_APPROVED, released, not yet on an RFQ), open RFQs (SENT / RESPONSES_IN with a response-tracker count), and awards awaiting approval or contract capture.
- **Data shown:** AWAITING SOURCING tile/list from `Requisition.identifier`, `Requisition.category`, `Requisition.purchaseType`, `Requisition.totalAmountInBase`, `Requisition.priority` (Badge), assignment date. OPEN RFQS list from `RFQ.reference`, `RFQ.requisitionId`, `RFQ.templateVariant`, `RFQ.status` (Badge), `RFQ.deadline` (with a late/overdue indicator vs now), invited count vs responded count (derived from `Quotation` rows per `RFQ.invitedSupplierIds`). AWARDS list from `Quotation.status` = SELECTED awaiting `award.approve`, and `Contract.status` = DRAFT awaiting capture.
- **Primary actions:** "Start RFQ" on an awaiting-sourcing requisition (-> S04.2; perm `rfq.create`, condition requisition FULLY_APPROVED and released to sourcing); "Release call-off" on a requisition the system flags framework-eligible (-> S04.6; perm `calloff.release`); row click -> RFQ detail / response tracker (S04.3) or comparison (S04.4).
- **Secondary actions / navigation:** faceted filter by category / purchaseType / status / deadline window; search by requisition identifier or RFQ reference; sort by deadline (default ascending, soonest first); links to the originating requisition (S02.3) and to supplier records (S06.3).
- **States to design:** empty ("No requisitions awaiting sourcing"), loading (skeleton tiles + table), populated, error, permission-denied (a non-buyer who reaches the route).
- **Shadcn components:** Card (KPI tiles for the three buckets), DataTable (TanStack Table) per bucket or Tabs, Badge (priority / RFQ status / deadline-overdue), Popover + Command (faceted filter), Button.
- **Acceptance criteria (believable done):** (1) only requisitions at FULLY_APPROVED released to sourcing appear in the awaiting-sourcing bucket (an unapproved or draft requisition never shows); (2) each open RFQ shows responded-vs-invited counts derived from real Quotation rows, not a static number; (3) an RFQ past its `RFQ.deadline` shows an overdue Badge; (4) a framework-eligible requisition surfaces "Release call-off" instead of (or alongside) "Start RFQ" only when an ACTIVE in-validity framework with remaining ceiling covers the line; (5) "Start RFQ" is gated by `rfq.create` and hidden for non-buyers.
- **Tags:** [S1.1 | AB handler assignment + RA released-on-approval]

### S04.2 Create RFQ and Invite Suppliers (archetype: wizard / form)
- **Route:** `/sourcing/rfq/new?requisitionId=:id`
- **Roles:** Procurement/Buyer (act: `rfq.create`, `rfq.invite`, `supplier.qualify`).
- **Realizes:** narrative steps 2 (define business need: internal target price, regulated flag, criticality), 5b (prequalify suppliers), 6 (invite to tender, float RFQ to >= 3 suppliers), and the auto-dispatch in step 7.
- **Purpose:** turn an approved requisition into a floated RFQ: confirm the need per line, set the internal benchmark that is never shown to a supplier, pick the prequalified suppliers, set the deadline, and dispatch unique-link invitations.
- **Data shown / captured:** HEADER from `RFQ.json` - `RFQ.reference` (auto, read-only), `RFQ.requisitionId` (read-only, from the query param), `RFQ.templateVariant` (Select: Import | Local | Service; defaults from requisition purchaseType/category), `RFQ.paymentTermsRequest` (Select, PaymentTerms enum), `RFQ.deliveryTermsRequest` (text), `RFQ.deadline` (datetime picker, must be after now/sentDate), `RFQ.internalTargetPrice` (money, INTERNAL ONLY, marked with a lock icon and a "never sent to suppliers" helper). LINES from `RFQ.lineItems` (read-only, copied from the requisition lines: description, quantity, uom, hsCode, needDate). PER-LINE need-definition fields surfaced from the narrative (needSpec, targetQuantity, regulatedItem flag, criticalityTier) shown on the line so the buyer confirms the structured need. SUPPLIER SELECTION: a Combobox/multi-select over `Supplier.name` filtered to qualified candidates (`Supplier.status` = ONBOARDED, valid `Supplier.certifications` for the category, `Supplier.avlScopeOfApproval` covering the line, not SUSPENDED/OFFBOARDED); selected ids populate `RFQ.invitedSupplierIds`.
- **Primary actions:** "Save Draft" (`RFQ.status` = DRAFT; no dispatch); "Send RFQ" (validates the invite rules, sets `RFQ.status` -> SENT, stamps `RFQ.sentDate`, dispatches unique-link email+OTP invitations per supplier; perm `rfq.invite`). "Add supplier" opens the qualified-supplier Combobox; a not-yet-onboarded lead links out to supplier onboarding (S06.2) and cannot be added until ONBOARDED.
- **Secondary actions / navigation:** remove an invited supplier; "justify fewer than three" note (mandatory to send when goods and invited count < 3); link back to the requisition (S02.3); the regulated/permit-expiry inline alert (see inline behaviours) on a regulated line.
- **States to design:** new (empty header, lines pre-filled), validation-error (deadline in the past, fewer than three suppliers for goods without justification, a non-onboarded supplier selected), saved-draft toast, sent toast/redirect to the response tracker (S04.3), permission-denied.
- **Shadcn components:** Stepper (Define need -> Invite suppliers -> Review and send) or Form with Card sections, Combobox (qualified suppliers), Select (template variant, payment terms), DateTimePicker (deadline), Input (internal target price with lock affordance), Table (read-only lines), Toast, Dialog (send confirmation).
- **Acceptance criteria (believable done):** (1) `RFQ.internalTargetPrice` renders with a lock affordance and "never sent to suppliers" copy, and is provably excluded from the dispatched payload (the supplier-facing preview/portal in supplier-portal shows no target price) - the one field whose non-exposure is a stated invariant; (2) the supplier Combobox lists only suppliers that are ONBOARDED with valid certifications and AVL scope covering the line; a SUSPENDED / OFFBOARDED / PENDING supplier cannot be selected; (3) "Send RFQ" enforces >= 3 invited suppliers for goods (`RFQ.invitedSupplierIds` count) and requires a justification note when fewer; services may send with 1-2 or a single provider; (4) `RFQ.deadline` must be after now; a past datetime is rejected inline; (5) on send, `RFQ.status` moves DRAFT -> SENT, `RFQ.sentDate` is stamped, and one invitation per supplier is dispatched (visible as a per-supplier row appearing on S04.3); (6) `RFQ.reference` is generated once and shown read-only.
- **Tags:** [S1.8 | ISO 8.4.1 | AB >=3 suppliers + internal target never sent]

### S04.3 RFQ Response Tracker (archetype: list / detail)
- **Route:** `/sourcing/rfq/:reference`
- **Roles:** Procurement/Buyer (act: capture manual quote as fallback, open comparison); Approver (view, in award review).
- **Realizes:** narrative steps 7-8 (invitations sent, supplier submits quotation), 10-11 (negotiate, supplier re-opens the same form), and the responsiveness tracking that feeds the scorecard.
- **Purpose:** show, per invited supplier, where its quote stands (invited / responded / under negotiation / revised / late), so the buyer knows when enough quotes are in to compare and which suppliers to chase.
- **Data shown:** RFQ header from `RFQ.reference`, `RFQ.status` (Badge), `RFQ.deadline`, `RFQ.templateVariant`. PER-INVITED-SUPPLIER rows: `Supplier.name`, response status derived from the matching `Quotation` (NOT RESPONDED if none, else `Quotation.status`: SUBMITTED | UNDER_NEGOTIATION | REVISED | SELECTED | REJECTED), `Quotation.submittedAt` and a late Badge when `submittedAt` > `RFQ.deadline`, `Quotation.revisedAt` (negotiation round indicator), `Quotation.price` + `Quotation.currency` once submitted.
- **Primary actions:** "Open comparison" (-> S04.4; enabled once at least the minimum quotes are in; perm `quote.compare`); "Enter quote manually" (manual fallback capture for a supplier, perm `quote.capture` conditional buyer fallback; opens a Sheet mirroring the supplier quote fields); "Mark under negotiation" on a submitted quote (`Quotation.status` SUBMITTED -> UNDER_NEGOTIATION); resend invitation to a non-responder.
- **Secondary actions / navigation:** link to each supplier record (S06.3); link to the requisition (S02.3); filter rows by response status.
- **States to design:** loading, populated (mix of responded and not-responded), empty (sent, none responded yet), error.
- **Shadcn components:** Card (RFQ header), DataTable (per-supplier response rows), Badge (response status, late, negotiation round), Sheet (manual quote entry), Button, Toast.
- **Acceptance criteria (believable done):** (1) every supplier in `RFQ.invitedSupplierIds` appears as a row, including non-responders shown as NOT RESPONDED (the tracker reflects the invited set, not just the quotes received); (2) a quote with `Quotation.submittedAt` after `RFQ.deadline` is Badged late; (3) the negotiation indicator reflects `Quotation.revisedAt` (a revised quote shows its round, same `Quotation.quotationId`); (4) "Open comparison" is gated by `quote.compare` and only enabled when there is at least one comparable quote; (5) manual quote entry is the documented buyer fallback (`quote.capture` conditional), not the primary path, and a manually entered quote is flagged as such.
- **Tags:** [S1.8 / S1.10 | RS cycle-time | AB unique-link + sole-editor]

### S04.4 Landed-Cost Comparison Workbench (archetype: comparison) - MARQUEE SCREEN
- **Route:** `/sourcing/rfq/:reference/compare`
- **Roles:** Procurement/Buyer (act: `quote.compare`, `supplier.select`, enter landed-cost actuals); Approver (view in award review); Quality (act on the regulated COA/MSDS gate inline, `qc.approve` / `qc.reject`).
- **Realizes:** narrative steps 12 (compute normalized landed cost), 13 (rank scorecard-weighted, colour-code, flag spikes), 14-15 (regulated COA/MSDS gate), 16 (select supplier, justify a non-top pick).
- **Purpose:** the single decision-support screen where the buyer compares quotes on a normalized laid-down cost rather than headline price, sees the recommendation reorder when freight and duty flip a cheap import, and is forced to justify deviating from the ranked top pick. This is the screen the storyboard names the #1 wow.
- **Data shown:** SIDE-BY-SIDE columns, one per quote. Per quote header: `Supplier.name`, `Supplier.avlGrade` (Badge), `Quotation.price` + `Quotation.currency`, `Quotation.paymentTerms`, `Quotation.incoterm`, `Quotation.transportMode`, `Quotation.submittedAt` (late Badge if past deadline). LANDED-COST BUILD-UP rows from `LandedCost.json`: `quote`, `freight`, `insurance`, `duty`, `exemption` (shown as a subtraction), `localPOD`, `localPOL`, `customsClearance`, `demurrage`, `misc`, `documentation`, then `landedTotal` and the comparison figure `landedTotalInBase`. `LandedCost.averagingFallbackUsed` shown as a per-component "estimated" marker. `LandedCost.damage` shown in a separate, clearly-labelled row that is NOT part of the total. RANKING/SCORE row: the scorecard-weighted composite (from `SupplierScorecard.composite` and the four sub-scores `deliveryScore` / `qualityScore` / `costScore` / `responsivenessScore` with the stored `SupplierScorecard.weights`), colour-coded green/amber/red per band, with the rank order driven by `landedTotalInBase` adjusted by the scorecard weighting. SPIKE FLAG on any quote where `Quotation.price` (or line `QuotationLine.unitPrice`) > 105% of last purchase price (marketPriceContext). SAVINGS line: worst-best spread and negotiation savings vs the baseline (last purchase price / `RFQ.internalTargetPrice`), reported not gated. A toggle to show per-line detail from `QuotationLine` (unitPrice, quantity, lineTotal, leadTime) so the comparison can be line-for-line.
- **Primary actions:** "Select supplier" per column (`supplier.select`, `quote.compare`; sets `Quotation.status` -> SELECTED; captures `selectedQuotationId`, optional `selectionRationale`, and MANDATORY `justificationForNonTopPick` when the chosen quote is not rank-1); "Approve COA/MSDS" / "Reject COA/MSDS" inline for a regulated line (Quality, `qc.approve` / `qc.reject`; reject requires a mandatory reason and disqualifies that quote for the regulated item); "Enter actuals" on a landed-cost component (buyer, recomputes `landedTotal` and re-ranks). Selection is a recommendation and does not bind until award approval (S04.5).
- **Secondary actions / navigation:** toggle headline-price vs landed-cost ranking (to demonstrate the reorder); toggle per-line view; sort columns by landed total or by composite score; link to each supplier record and scorecard (S06.3 / 12-analytics); "Recompare" return path from a sent-back award.
- **States to design:** loading (skeleton columns), populated (>= 2 quotes), too-few-quotes (one quote, comparison shown with a fewer-than-three justification note for goods), regulated-gate-pending (Select disabled on a regulated quote until Quality approves), error (FX unavailable -> degrade to `landedTotal`, see inline), permission-denied.
- **Shadcn components:** bespoke side-by-side Table with a highlighted best-value column, Badge (AVL grade, spike flag, late, gate status, averaging-fallback "estimated"), Card (per-quote header), Dialog (selection with justification textarea), Inline editable cells or Sheet (landed-cost actuals), Tooltip (formula breakdown), Toast, Tabs/Toggle (price vs landed, summary vs per-line).
- **Acceptance criteria (believable done):** (1) ranking sorts on `landedTotalInBase` (scorecard-weighted), NOT on `Quotation.price`; toggling price-vs-landed visibly reorders the columns so the cheapest unit price is not the top pick when freight + duty flip it (the demonstrable reorder, the core wow); (2) `LandedCost.landedTotal` renders exactly the AB formula `quote + freight + insurance + duty - exemption + localPOD + localPOL + customsClearance + demurrage + misc + documentation`, with `exemption` subtracted, and `LandedCost.damage` shown separately and provably EXCLUDED from the total (axiom A14); (3) a quote whose price exceeds 105% of last purchase price shows the abnormal-price-spike Badge (> 5% threshold), and savings are shown as reported figures, never as a gate; (4) the "Select supplier" action is DISABLED until a `justificationForNonTopPick` is entered whenever the chosen quote is not rank-1 (the non-top-pick rule), and enabled with no justification for the rank-1 pick; (5) for a regulated line (`regulatedItem` = true), Select is hard-blocked on that quote until Quality records `qc.approve` of COA/MSDS; a `qc.reject` with a mandatory reason disqualifies that quote (`Quotation.status` -> REJECTED) and the buyer sources an alternative; (6) any landed-cost component drawn from the past-order average is marked (`LandedCost.averagingFallbackUsed`) so the buyer sees which parts are provisional, and a missing charge never blocks the comparison; (7) cross-currency quotes compare on `landedTotalInBase` (FX-normalized); on FX failure the screen degrades to `landedTotal` with a "rate unavailable" note rather than blocking (axiom A12); (8) colour-coding (green/amber/red) follows the scorecard bands and compliance is shown as a pass/fail gate, never folded into the weighted composite (axiom A15).
- **Tags:** [S1.9 | ISO 8.6 / 8.4.1 | AB landed-cost reorder + >5% spike + non-top-pick justification + QC gate]

### S04.5 Award and Capture Contract (archetype: form / detail)
- **Route:** `/sourcing/rfq/:reference/award`
- **Roles:** Procurement/Buyer (act: `contract.manage`; capture contract after award approval); Approver (act: `award.approve` or RECOMPARE; SoD: not the selecting buyer); Management (conditional award approve).
- **Realizes:** narrative steps 17 (award selection approved? RECOMPARE loop), 18 (award and capture contract terms incl. renewal date), 19 (ready for PO).
- **Purpose:** route the selected quote through award approval with segregation of duties, then capture the contract that locks the supplier and quote as the source of truth for the PO and schedules the renewal reminder.
- **Data shown / captured:** the selected `Quotation` summary (supplier, landed total, terms, incoterm) and the comparison rank with any `justificationForNonTopPick`. APPROVAL: the award stage status from `approvalCompletion` (AWAITING_APPROVAL -> APPROVED) with the approver and SoD note (award approver != selecting buyer). CONTRACT from `Contract.json` - `Contract.contractId` (auto, read-only), `Contract.supplierId` (from award), `Contract.requisitionId` (from award), `Contract.awardedQuotationId` (from award), `Contract.terms` (textarea, seeded from the quote), `Contract.scopeOfWork` (textarea, mandatory for service/contract awards), `Contract.agreedPrices` (price list, seeded from the awarded quotation lines), `Contract.validityStart`, `Contract.validityEnd`, `Contract.renewalDate` (drives the 15-day-before reminder), `Contract.status` (Badge).
- **Primary actions:** "Approve award" (Approver, `award.approve`; `approvalCompletion` AWAITING_APPROVAL -> APPROVED; guard: actor is the requested award approver or a qualified assignee AND NOT the selecting buyer, axiom A6); "Send back to recompare" (Approver; `awardDecision` = RECOMPARE with a mandatory recompareNote; returns to S04.4); "Capture contract" / "Award" (Buyer, `contract.manage`; `Contract.status` DRAFT -> ACTIVE; locks the supplier/quote and emits the PO-ready package); "Mark ready for PO" handover to flow 05.
- **Secondary actions / navigation:** return to comparison (S04.4); link to the supplier (S06.3) and requisition (S02.3); link forward to the PO create screen (S05.2) once PO-ready.
- **States to design:** awaiting-award-approval (Buyer sees read-only, Approver sees Approve/Recompare), recompare-returned, contract-capture (post-approval), awarded/PO-ready, permission-denied (the selecting buyer attempting to approve their own award - blocked by SoD).
- **Shadcn components:** Card (selected-quote and approval summary), Form (contract fields), DatePicker (validity, renewal), Textarea (terms, scope), Dialog (recompare with mandatory note), Badge (award stage, contract status), Button group, Toast.
- **Acceptance criteria (believable done):** (1) award approval reuses the approval engine (`approvalCompletion` AWAITING_APPROVAL -> APPROVED) and the selecting buyer cannot approve their own award (SoD, axiom A6) - the self-approval action does not render for that user; (2) RECOMPARE requires a mandatory note and returns to S04.4 for re-rank / re-negotiate / re-select (multiple rounds possible); (3) the contract cannot be captured until the award is APPROVED; (4) `Contract.renewalDate` schedules a 15-day-before renewal reminder and `Contract.status` moves DRAFT -> ACTIVE on award; (5) `Contract.agreedPrices` is seeded from the awarded quotation lines so the PO line price cannot drift from the awarded price; (6) `scopeOfWork` is mandatory for a service/contract award and optional for a goods award.
- **Tags:** [S1.10 / OE6 | AB contract capture + 15-day renewal + SoD]

### S04.6 Framework Call-Off Release (archetype: form / modal)
- **Route:** `/sourcing/call-off/new?requisitionId=:id`
- **Roles:** Procurement/Buyer (act: `calloff.release`); Requester and Engineering conditional (`calloff.release` against framework per matrix); Administrator.
- **Realizes:** narrative step 5a (release call-off against framework / preferred supplier - the competitive-event bypass).
- **Purpose:** release a requisition against an active framework without re-sourcing, drawing down the ceiling and inheriting agreed price and terms read-only, producing an award-equivalent record straight to PO-ready.
- **Data shown / captured:** from `CallOffRelease.json` - `CallOffRelease.frameworkAgreementId` (Select over ACTIVE in-validity frameworks covering the line), `CallOffRelease.requisitionId` (read-only, from the query param), `CallOffRelease.quantity` (entered, > 0), `CallOffRelease.agreedPrice` (read-only from framework), `CallOffRelease.value` (computed quantity x agreedPrice), `CallOffRelease.valueInBase` (computed), `CallOffRelease.paymentTerms` / `deliveryTerms` / `incoterm` (read-only from framework), `CallOffRelease.status` (Badge). Framework headroom from `FrameworkAgreement.totalValueCeiling` / `consumedValue` and `totalQuantityCeiling` / `consumedQuantity` (remaining shown).
- **Primary actions:** "Release call-off" (`calloff.release`; `CallOffRelease.status` -> RELEASED then PO_READY; draws down the framework ceiling; emits the award-equivalent package). Guard: framework ACTIVE, within validity, remaining ceiling >= this draw.
- **Secondary actions / navigation:** link to the framework record; link to the requisition (S02.3); cancel (restores any drawn-down ceiling).
- **States to design:** populated (framework selected, headroom shown), over-ceiling (release blocked, see acceptance criteria), framework-expired/suspended-supplier (release blocked), released/PO-ready, permission-denied.
- **Shadcn components:** Form, Select (framework), Input (quantity), read-only fields (price/terms), Card (ceiling headroom with a progress bar), Badge (call-off status), Toast, Dialog (release confirmation).
- **Acceptance criteria (believable done):** (1) price, payment terms, delivery terms, and incoterm are inherited READ-ONLY from `FrameworkAgreement.agreedPrices` and cannot be edited on the call-off; (2) a release whose cumulative draw-down (`consumedQuantity` + this, `consumedValue` + this) would exceed the `totalQuantityCeiling` / `totalValueCeiling` is BLOCKED with a clear message and the buyer runs a competitive event instead; (3) a release is permitted only when the framework is ACTIVE and within validity and the supplier is not SUSPENDED (axiom A10); (4) the call-off skips RFQ / quotation / negotiation / comparison and produces a PO-ready package straight away (no competitive approval, SoD still applies to value-changing edits); (5) `CallOffRelease.value` recomputes as quantity x the read-only agreed price.
- **Tags:** [S1.3 / OE6 | data-model FrameworkAgreement + CallOffRelease]

### Inline edge behaviours shown on these screens (not separate screens)
- **Internal target price never exposed (sourcing invariant):** on S04.2 `RFQ.internalTargetPrice` is captured with a lock affordance and is provably absent from the dispatched supplier payload and the supplier-portal quote form; it only seeds the savings baseline (S04.4) and the spike flag.
- **Incoterm validated against transport mode:** on the supplier quote (supplier-portal) and on any S04.3 manual fallback entry, `Quotation.incoterm` is validated against `Quotation.transportMode` at every submit/revise (FOB/CIF/CFR sea-only; air/road/courier/multimodal use FCA/CPT/CIP/DAP; EXW mode-agnostic); an invalid combination is rejected with a validation message and the value is corrected before the quote enters the comparison.
- **Abnormal price spike (> 5%):** on S04.4 a quote price more than 5% above last purchase price shows a spike Badge; it flags, never blocks.
- **Averaging fallback for missing landed-cost inputs:** on S04.4 any missing charge falls back to the past-order average and is marked in `LandedCost.averagingFallbackUsed`; a comparison is never blocked by a missing charge; damage is tracked separately and excluded from the total.
- **Regulated permit-expiry alert:** on S04.2 a regulated chemical line surfaces a permit-expiry / quantity alert (about one week before expiry) to head and handler at supplier/line selection.
- **Currency degradation (e07):** on S04.4 if FX conversion fails, `landedTotalInBase` degrades to `landedTotal` with a "rate unavailable" note rather than blocking the comparison (axiom A12).

### Per-role landing covered
S04.1 is the Buyer landing (storyboard section 6).
