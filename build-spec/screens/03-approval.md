# Screens: 03 Approval Workflow (Build-real)

Flow 03 realized as screens. Build-real depth: the approval queue, the per-requisition approve / return-for-revision action, and the Budget Owner override decision are interactive and seeded; auto-approval, nearest-bucket selection, and the financial-edit revert show as system-marked badges and inline behaviours (no separate human screen). Source: `documentation/03-approval.md`, `mvp-and-storyboard.md` (golden-path beat 2: one stage auto-approves, one needs a human, SoD visible), `model/role-permission-matrix.md`, state machine `approvalCompletion`, data dictionary `ApprovalStageCompletion.json` / `Requisition.json` / `RoutingRule.json` / `Budget.json` / `User.json`.

The engine is one machine serving requisition, PO, award, supplier-onboarding, and payment-schedule approvals; these screens are scoped to the requisition case for the demo. Every state-changing action maps to an `approvalCompletion` transition and a permission; SoD (never approve own record, A6) is enforced on every approve action; all thresholds compare `totalAmountInBase` (A12); every decision, assignment, and revert is audited and broadcast (A13).

### S03.1 Approval Queue (archetype: queue / Approver landing with KPI tiles)
- **Route:** `/approvals` (the Approver landing; aliased as S00.3).
- **Roles:** Approver (in-chain, act: `approval.approve`, `approval.reject`); Management (final stage, act); Buyer (`approval.requestApproval` only, conditional); others view per matrix.
- **Realizes:** the Approver "first 30 seconds" (storyboard 6); narrative steps 10-11 entry point, the resolved approver opens the work waiting on them.
- **Purpose:** show every stage waiting on this approver, oldest and most urgent first, with the amount and SoD status visible before any drill-down.
- **Data shown:** KPI tiles: pending count (completions where this user is `approvalRequestedToId` or a qualified assignee and `completionStatus==AWAITING_APPROVAL`), overdue count (remainingTime <= 0 from `Requisition.priority` allowed duration vs elapsed), total `Requisition.totalAmountInBase` awaiting this approver, auto-approved-today count (informational; those never required a human). Queue rows: `Requisition.identifier`, `ApprovalStageCompletion.stage` (REQ_DEPARTMENT/PROCUREMENT/BUDGET/FINANCE/MANAGEMENT), requester `User.name`, `category`, `priority` (Badge), `totalAmountInBase`, `completionStatus` (Badge), aging badge, and an SoD indicator on any row whose record the viewer requested/buys (own record).
- **Primary actions:** row -> opens the per-requisition approval action (S03.2). The actionable transitions originate there: "Approve" (`approve`, AWAITING_APPROVAL -> APPROVED; perm `approval.approve`; guard actor == `approvalRequestedToId` or a qualified assignee, AND SoD actor != record `requesterId`/`buyerId`, A6), "Return for revision" (`returnForRevision`, AWAITING_APPROVAL -> IN_PROGRESS, mandatory note; perm `approval.reject`).
- **Secondary actions / navigation:** filter by stage/department/aging/category; sort by amount or age; "Reassign" a stage to another eligible user (perm `approval.reassign`; guard assigner designation rank >= the stage's `requiredDesignationRank` and the completion not yet APPROVED); row click also links to S02.3 (requisition detail).
- **States to design:** empty ("Nothing awaiting your approval."), loading (tile + table skeleton), populated, permission-denied.
- **Shadcn:** Card KPI tiles, DataTable, Badge (stage, completionStatus, aging green/amber/red, SoD self-record, auto-approved), Popover + Command (filter), Button, Dialog/Sheet (opens S03.2), Toast.
- **Acceptance criteria:** (1) rows are only those where this user is `approvalRequestedToId` or a qualified assignee AND `completionStatus==AWAITING_APPROVAL`, so no APPROVED, NOT_STARTED, or auto-approved-only rows appear as actionable; (2) any row whose record this user requested or buys shows a disabled action with reason "Cannot approve your own record" (SoD A6) on the landing itself; (3) the aging badge computes from `priority` allowed duration vs elapsed (ASAP/SameDay/Within2Days/Within1Week), so overdue rows render red; (4) auto-approved stages are not actionable rows (they never reached AWAITING for a human) and are counted only in the informational tile; (5) reassignment is gated by designation rank (A5) and blocked on an APPROVED stage.
- **Tags:** [OE2 | RA + storyboard 6]

### S03.2 Requisition Approval Action: Approve / Return for Revision (archetype: modal, Sheet/Dialog)
- **Route:** opened over `/approvals/:requisitionId/:stage` (a Sheet on the queue, or a Dialog from S02.3 requisition detail).
- **Roles:** Approver (act: `approval.approve` / `approval.reject`); Management at the MANAGEMENT stage.
- **Realizes:** narrative steps 10-13: the approver reviews the record and decides; on approve the stage records APPROVED and routing advances; on return the record loops back to the requester with a mandatory note.
- **Purpose:** the single decision surface for one stage of one requisition: full context plus the two legal decisions, SoD- and permission-gated.
- **Data shown:** the requisition header and lines, read-only: `Requisition.identifier`, requester `User.name`, `category`, `directOrIndirect`, `purchaseType`, `priority`, `totalAmount` (record currency) and `totalAmountInBase` (the value thresholds compared against, A12), `projectOrCostCenterId`, `justification`; the line list (item, quantity, unitPrice, needDate). The approval context: the current `ApprovalStageCompletion.stage`, `completionStatus`, `assignedToId`, `approvalRequestedToId`, `approverLimit`, prior stage decisions (who approved each prior stage and when, or the auto-approved badge), and prior `returnNote`s from the immutable activity log (`Requisition.remarks`). A budget strip when the cost-center is over budget (`totalAmountInBase > Budget.availableAmount`) flags it before the decision.
- **Primary actions:**
  - "Approve" (`approve`, AWAITING_APPROVAL -> APPROVED; perm `approval.approve`). Guard: acting user == `approvalRequestedToId` OR a qualified assignee of sufficient `requiredDesignationRank`; AND SoD: acting user != record `requesterId` and != `buyerId` (A6). Effects: `completionStatus=APPROVED`, `approvedById`=actor, `approvedAt`=now, route to the next stage (handleRouting), update the approver OTIF/streak stats (manual approval only). The Approve button is disabled with reason "Cannot approve your own record" when SoD fails.
  - "Return for revision" (`returnForRevision`, AWAITING_APPROVAL -> IN_PROGRESS; perm `approval.reject`). Mandatory `returnNote` (non-empty to submit). Effect: back to the requester; the record re-enters the chain at the first stage on resubmit; the identifier is immutable and unchanged across the revision (A1). The Submit-return button is disabled until the note is non-empty.
- **Secondary actions / navigation:** open the full requisition detail (S02.3); view the full activity log; "Reassign" (perm `approval.reassign`, rank-gated) if the approver cannot decide.
- **States to design:** loading (fetch record + completion), populated (decision-ready), SoD-blocked (Approve disabled with the self-record reason), validation-error (empty return note on a return attempt), submitted (toast + queue refresh), permission-denied (not the resolved approver and not a qualified assignee).
- **Shadcn:** Sheet (side editor over the queue) or Dialog (confirm from detail), Card (header summary), Table (lines, read-only), Badge (stage, completionStatus, auto-approved on prior stages, over-budget, SoD), Textarea (mandatory return note), Button group (Approve / Return), Alert (over-budget strip), Toast.
- **Acceptance criteria:** (1) Approve is permission-gated (`approval.approve`) AND SoD-gated, disabled with "Cannot approve your own record" when the acting user is the record's requester or buyer (A6); auto-approval is the only no-second-person path; (2) Return requires a non-empty `returnNote` to submit (mandatory note); (3) on approve, the stage records `approvedById` + `approvedAt` and routing advances to the next not-APPROVED completion (handleRouting); a manual approve updates the OTIF streak, an auto-approval does not; (4) prior auto-approved stages display the system "Auto-approved" badge with the bucket approver and limit, distinct from manual approvals; (5) the identifier is shown and is unchanged across a return-and-resubmit (A1); (6) thresholds and the budget strip use `totalAmountInBase` (A12).
- **Tags:** [OE2 | ISO 8.4 | RA approve/return + SoD + AB reject-reason-mandatory]

### S03.3 Budget Stage Decision: Approve / Override / Return (archetype: modal, Sheet/Dialog, Budget Owner)
- **Route:** opened over `/approvals/:requisitionId/budget` (a Sheet from the Budget Owner landing S00.11, or a Dialog from S02.3).
- **Roles:** Budget Owner (act: `budget.approve`, scope own cost-center).
- **Realizes:** narrative steps 6-7: the Budget Owner reviews commitment vs available budget and decides approve, approve-override, or return. This is the SOFT check; the hard commit/encumbrance happens at PO issue (flow 05).
- **Purpose:** the Budget Owner's decision surface for the configurable BUDGET stage, with the over-budget exception logged, never silently bypassed.
- **Data shown (read-only budget context, A12 base currency):** `Budget.costCenterId`, `period`, `amount`, `availableAmount`, `committedAmount`, `actualAmount`; this requisition's `totalAmountInBase`; the projected available-after-commit (`availableAmount - totalAmountInBase`); the requisition `identifier`, requester `User.name`, `category`, `justification`. An over-budget flag when `totalAmountInBase > availableAmount`.
- **Primary actions:**
  - "Approve budget" (`approve` the BUDGET completion, AWAITING_APPROVAL -> APPROVED; perm `budget.approve`). Available when `totalAmountInBase <= availableAmount`.
  - "Approve override" (`approve` with an override; sets `Requisition.budgetOverride` = {approvedBy, reason, at} and `Budget` over-commit per the logged override; then APPROVED; perm `budget.approve`). Mandatory `overrideReason` when `availableAmount < totalAmountInBase`; the button is disabled until a non-empty reason is entered. The override is logged, never a silent bypass (A4).
  - "Return for revision" (`returnForRevision`, AWAITING_APPROVAL -> IN_PROGRESS; mandatory `returnNote`; perm `approval.reject`). The requisition goes back to the requester to reduce or re-scope.
- **Secondary actions / navigation:** open the budget's commitment ledger (e02); open the requisition detail (S02.3).
- **States to design:** loading, populated (within budget, only Approve + Return show), over-budget (Approve hidden/disabled; Override requires a reason; Return available), validation-error (empty override reason on an over-budget override, or empty return note), submitted (toast), permission-denied (not the cost-center's Budget Owner).
- **Shadcn:** Sheet/Dialog, Card (budget figures with available/committed/actual bars), Badge (over-budget red, within-budget green), Textarea (override reason / return note), Button group (Approve / Approve override / Return), Alert (over-budget warning), Toast.
- **Acceptance criteria:** (1) the budget check is soft, an over-budget requisition is not blocked; it routes to this decision, and the only over-budget approval path is "Approve override" with a mandatory reason (A4); (2) "Approve override" writes `Requisition.budgetOverride` with approver + reason + at (the logged exception an auditor inspects), never a silent over-commit; (3) all figures are base-currency normalized (A12); (4) within budget, only "Approve budget" and "Return" render (no override path); (5) this is the soft check; the screen states that the hard encumbrance happens at PO issue (flow 05), not here; (6) scoped to the cost-center's Budget Owner (`Project.managerId`); a non-owner gets permission-denied.
- **Tags:** [OE11 | build-new budget service + AB over-budget override | platform-services 11]

### Inline edge behaviours shown on these screens (not separate screens)
- **Auto-approval as a system-marked badge (no human screen):** when a stage auto-approves (`allowAutoApproval==true` and `totalAmountInBase <= effectiveApproverLimit`, default limit 200000, inclusive, A7), it never appears as an actionable queue row. On S02.3 (requisition detail, Approvals tab) and in S03.2's prior-decisions panel it renders an "Auto-approved" badge with `isAutoApproved==true`, the recorded nearest-bucket `approvalRequestedToId`, and the `approverLimit` used, with `approvedById` null. No approval-requested email is sent and the OTIF stats path does not run. The golden path shows this on the low-value carton line's FINANCE stage.
- **Nearest-bucket selection (inline, no dedicated UI):** the system reserves high-limit approvers for large amounts by selecting the minimum-sufficient limit bucket, tie-broken least-loaded. This is described in S03.1/S03.2 copy as how the resolved `approvalRequestedToId` and `approverLimit` were chosen; the limit shown on an auto-approved badge is that bucket's limit. The high-value API line's stage selects a human approver because no bucket within limit auto-approves it.
- **Financial-edit revert (inline behaviour on S02.3 and the queue):** after a write that changed `payTerms` or `paymentSchedules`, the system reverts every completion where `stage IN {FINANCE, MANAGEMENT}` AND `completionStatus==APPROVED` AND `isAutoApproved==true` back to AWAITING_APPROVAL, increments `revertedCount`, and re-queues those stages (`financialRevert`, APPROVED -> AWAITING_APPROVAL, A7). Manual approvals and REQ_DEPARTMENT/PROCUREMENT are never reverted. On S02.3 the reverted stage shows a "Reverted, re-approval needed" badge with the `revertedCount`; the stage reappears in S03.1 for a fresh decision. The hook never aborts the originating write.
- **No-routing-rule / no-eligible-approver stalls (inline):** if chain resolution finds no matching `RoutingRule` for (department, stage) or an empty eligible approver pool, the requisition stalls IN_PROGRESS with a "No routing rule" / "No eligible approver" notice on S02.3, pointing the Administrator to S01.3 to fix coverage. No partial chain is created.
